Inspiring Young Women to Become the Mathematical Leaders of Tomorrow

MEOW is a two-week summer camp focused on advanced mathematics, designed for rising 6th to 9th graders. The camp will run three days a week for seven hours per day (9AM to 4PM) at the Bethesda Chevy-Chase Service Center, with each session divided into a learning phase and a project phase. Participants will explore cryptography, fractals, and graph theory, gaining both theoretical understanding and hands-on experience.

Throughout the camp, students will work in three groups: Fractals, Graph Theory, and Cryptography. Each group is tasked with a different project to accomplish an overarching goal: let three friends (Amber, Megan, and Paige) enjoy their time at the Dragon Curve Amusement Park. The Graph Theory group will learn all about introductory graph theory, with a final project on optimizing the trio's schedule. The Cryptography group will apply modular arithmetic, ciphers, and prime factorization to decrypt messages and uncover clues to help protect the amusement park from bandits. Finally, the Fractal Art Group will focus on creating intricate fractal designs, using self-similarity and recursion to design the amusement park rides and attractions.. All students will also create presentations on what they have learned throughout the camp.

On the final day, all groups will come together for the MEOW symposium, where students present their work to an open audience. FAQ

How many people will be accepted into MEOW?

MEOW will accept ~15 students into our program this year. We hope to expand to a larger program into future!

Who is the camp taught by?

MEOW will be taught by Paige Zhu and Amber Weng, both of whom are qualified high schoolers. To learn more about our tutors, click this link.

Where will MEOW meet?

MEOW will meet at the Bethesda Chevy-Chase Service Center.

4805 Edgemoor Lane - Bethesda, Maryland 20814

What level of math should my child be at to participate?

Our camp is inclusive for all, but is primarily targeted towards students who previously already have a math background in MATHCOUNTS or other middle school math competitions.

What are the age requirements to apply for MEOW?

To apply for MEOW, you must be a rising 6th-9th grader.

Do you need to pay for classes?

Our goal is to make MEOW as free as possible. However, it is likely that you will need to pay a deposit to participate in this camp.

What is the admission style for the application?

We are accepting students on a rolling basis admission style, meaning that students will be accepted as applications come in.
